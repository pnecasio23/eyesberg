GLACIERS

What exactly are Glaciers? In 1910, the glaciers found on earth were around 150 and recently it has been counted to only less than 30. Not only that, these glaciers have lessened to about two thirds in their area. In a few years, there might not even be any glaciers. These would’ve already melted and drawn up the sea level by a lot and thus might have already submerged some islands. See more.

How do they form? When snow stays in the same place for nearly the whole year, it can compile to make ice. This ice can then be the factor by which glaciers form. Recrystallization is when snow is compressed. When this occurs, the grain grows in size and it's shape becomes similar to that of sugar. Overtime, the snow will compact and become more dense. After about two years, this turns into firn - midway phase between snow and glaciers. It's density at this point is about two-thirds of water's density. This process continues on and on. The resulting product is a glacier. Since their density is greater than that of water, they float on oceans. Compaction and recrystallization of small particles to large ones such as the glaciers takes is a long process, therefore it takes them on average hundreds of years to form. See more.

Fun facts! Did you know that the largest glacier in the world is Lambert-Fisher Glacier which is found in Antarctica? If this whole glacier were to collapse, it could rise earth's sea level by 60 meters! See more.
