Awareness of Global Warming: PSHS-MC Students

Survey. Based on a survey we gave, PSHS students are not well aware about the causes of global warming. Most were aware about the causes of the harmful gases produced by factories and cars to the environment. A lot of people didn’t know much about the cause of excessive use of water to global warming; some were even clueless!

Glaciers as a cause of global warming. Of all the surveyees, 45.5% said they knew a lot about it. 45.5% said they knew just enough, and 9% said they knew little about it. Based on the question we gave them (“What percentage of the snows of Kilimanjaro do you think have melted since 1912 to this day?”), only 22.7% got the right answer which is “80%” See more. 22.7% is far from the 45.5% that said they knew a lot about this topic.

Deforestation as a cause of global warming. One said they were an expert in that field, 54.5% said they knew a lot about it, and 41.9% said they knew just enough about this. We asked our participants one question: Around how many acres of forest do you think are lost each year? 72.7% answered correctly: 18 000 000 acres! Could you imagine that? 18 million acres are lost every year this generation, what more could the next generation loose? See more.

Trash as a cause of global warming. Here we go, where many of the surveyees said they are aware about it a lot. It is surprising that at an average, around 15% of all waste is being recycled. Among the answers of our participants, 27.3% got the right answer! 15% is just a few, what happens to the other 85%? Well, some go to the rivers, oceans, forests, and many habitats of many animals. Imagine yourself living in a dumpster, would you live long enough? See more.

Carbon monoxide (CO) as a cause of global warming. Carbon monoxide naturally exists - some from volcanoes and bushfires. It could naturally occur to be around 0.2 ppm only which isn’t harmful to humans ¹, but what happens when we add up all the carbon monoxide produced artificially and naturally? Well that would be a ton. Natural CO emissions take up only 40% of the total CO in the atmosphere, while the artificial one’s take up 60% ²! Knowing these, we must use less of the inventions that produce a lot of CO such as vehicles, factories, cigarettes, etc.

Among the answers from the survey, 9.1% said forest fires had the most contribution to the amount of CO in the atmosphere, 13.6% said transportation, and 77.3% said factories and industries. Surprisingly, transportation had the most contribution to the production of CO³ . Nowadays, a lot of people own cars meaning a lot more CO is being produced. See more.
