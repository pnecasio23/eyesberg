How do we terraform Mars?
By Paul Necasio (self-written)
Published: 10 December 2019
Updated: 15 December 2019

TERRAFORMING AND COLONIZING MARS

Have you ever dreamed living outside this planet? Since before the space age, humans have always dreamed what goes on beyond the sky. As the landing of the moon began, we had a glance of the shining stars, the empty darkness, the colors of the nebulas, and other worlds that we can step on. We became curious about the existence of other beings around and beyond the solar system. Conspiracy theories develop and our imagination wandered around the planets. But what if we stop dreaming and start something? The earth is dying and we need to take action fast. One thing that we can do is to find a new home. The closest we can go now is Mars but it might not be that easy. In this page, we will talk about how we plan to terraform Mars to a living home and how to colonize it.

How do we terraform Mars? Mars, as we know is a red, harsh, and cold planet; contrasting with our green, lushful, well-heated planet. How can we go there if this is the environment that will face? One way to convert Mars to our home is through terraforming. Terraforming or terraformation of a planet, moon, or any celestial body is the hypothetical process of deliberately modifying its atmosphere, temparature, topology, and ecology to make it similar to Earth so it can support life. But before that, check out the problems we might face in our journey to terraforming Mars: See more

Phase One - Warming The Planet After securing money, resources, and protections for the harsh Martian environment, we can start the first phase of terraforming Mars. Here are the steps for warming the planet:

We'd begin by detonating thermonuclear weapons over the polar ice caps.

This would release carbon dioxide trapped inside dry ice on the surface.

The resulting greenhouse effect will trap sunlight thus, heating the planet and melting the polar ice more.

Gases and sunlight trapped would thicken the atmosphere.

Phase Two - Watering The Planet As ice melts, water comes out and flows through the planet. Water can be used to create weather for the planet

We'd first install giant orbital mirrors to focus sunlight over permafrost on the surface.

As the water ice melts, planetary seas would begin to form across the planet.

Rainwater would fall from the sky as a result of the efforts to generate an atmosphere.

Icy asteroids could also be redirected toward the planet's surface, melting on impact.

Phase Three - Fertilizing The Planet As water rises from the dry environment, first biological creatures will fertilize the soil to support plant growth.

On Earth, we would develop genetically engineered algae, bacteria, and plants.

These organisms would be deployed to Mars to create organic soil and release oxygen.

One day, we can 3D print these lifeforms using biological converter if we improve our technology.

Once oxygen levels normalize, we can start planting fruits, vegetables, and wheat to invest in agriculture

Phase Four - Populating The Planet Plant life is now prosper. The last challenge now is to populate Mars with us.

Robots will first arrive in the planet to prepare samples and to prepare human arrival.

Houses and structures will be 3D printed so transportation costs will be reduced

As basic infastractures are built, the first wave of humans will arrive.

A genetically diverse people (about 10,000) will be sent to Mars. They would be mutated to combat risks of diseases and plagues.
