Beyond the Stars

Humanity has been on planet Earth for quite some time, draining its resources for the greater good, or so they say. With our population going out of control and with our resources becoming scarcer, scientists began to see beyond the stars above us to find another planet like Earth.

Finding another planet just like Earth is uncommon with how precise the position Earth is to allow life to flourish: not too near to be neither burnt by the sun’s rays nor too far to be frozen by the extremely low temperature. The major factor for finding one potential habitable planet is to find stars with planets orbiting within its circumstellar habitable zone (CHZ), a zone wherein planets can support liquids, mainly water, with its sufficient atmospheric pressure. This zone varies in distance depending on the size of the star: The larger and hotter a star is, the further the habitable zone is.

However for the past few years, NASA, using the Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS), have discovered multiple exoplanets, planets that existed outside the Solar System, which are possible habitable planets, such as Proxima Centauri b, Gliese 667 Cc, Kepler – 442b, and much, much more. This is possible with the technology we have acquired and developed for decades. Despite the technology we have, humanity is still incapable of exploring beyond our Solar System, just like in Star Trek or Mass Effect. However we are not that far, in a few years we may be having space ships exploring beyond our Solar System.

When the day when we can explore the Solar System and beyond it with ease, and explore beyond it, it simply is dangerous. We may indeed know where meteors and comets go however we are still unsure of another life form. With such a topic still up for debate, all we can do right now is wait until time is right. Like what they say, “Time will tell.”
