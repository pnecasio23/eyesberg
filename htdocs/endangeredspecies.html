CRITICALLY ENDANGERED SPECIES

Will animals last forever? Animals have been on Earth for millions of years. Evolution helped them to hunt and survive better. Animals such as dinosaurs once ruled the Earth but became extinct because of a catastrophe. Soon, mammals arrived. Generations after generations, new animals rule and die out. Now, humans rule the Earth other creatures are inferior to us. This lead to extinction of such creatures. Some animals and plants are drastically decreasing in population due to loss of habitat mainly done by humans. These creatures who are in the brink of extinction are called "endangered species". In this article, we will look at the top 7 most endangered species on Earth and how can we help them move away from extinction.

  1. Amur Leopard

    2. Scientific Name:

    Pantera pardus orientalis

    Population:

    84

    Status:

    Critically endangered

    Location/s:

    far east Russia, North Korea, Northeast China

    Threats:

    Poaching, habitat destruction, inbreeding

    Fact:

    For camouflage in the snow, their coat is paler than other leopard subspecies.

  2. Sumatran Rhino

    3. Scientific Name:

    Dicerorhinus sumatrensis

    Population:

    83

    Status:

    Critically endangered

    Location/s:

    Sumatra, New Guinea

    Threats:

    Poaching, habitat destruction

    Fact:

    It is the smallest and the hairiest of the of the rhino family.

  3. Javan Rhino

    4. Scientific Name:

    Rhinoceros sondaicus

    Population:

    68

    Status:

    Critically endangered

    Location/s:

    Java

    Threats:

    Poaching

    Fact:

    Javan rhinoceros has grey or brown-grey hairless skin with pinkish skin on the folds, which are thick and create appearance of armored plates.

  4. Spix's Macaw

    5. Scientific Name:

    Cyanopsitta spixii

    Population:

    58

    Status:

    Extinct in the wild

    Location/s:

    Brazil

    Threats:

    Poaching, habitat destruction

    Fact:

    It is a small, elegant parrot with delicate blue-gray plumage, bright blue wings and tail, and an ash-blue crown.

  5. South China Tiger

    6. Scientific Name:

    Panthera tigris amoyensis

    Population:

    less than 50

    Status:

    Extinct in the wild

    Location/s:

    China

    Threats:

    Poaching, habitat destruction, inbreeding

    Fact:

    The males weigh about 330 pounds, and the females weight about 240 pounds. Their smaller size allows them to easily move through the dense jungles that they inhabit.

  6. Black Softshell Turtle

    7. Scientific Name:

    Nilssonia nigricans

    Population:

    44

    Status:

    Extinct in the wild

    Location/s:

    India, Bangladesh

    Threats:

    Habitat destruction

    Fact:

    Like other softshells, this turtle has a leathery shell and a snorkel-like nose.

  7. Vaquita

    8. Scientific Name:

    Phocoena sinus

    Population:

    30

    Status:

    Critically endangered

    Location/s:

    Gulf of California

    Threats:

    Fishing

    Fact:

    Vaquita is the smallest porpoise that belongs to the order Cetacea.
